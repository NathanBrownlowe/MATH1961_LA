    Remark 1.5.2.

    We call the \(+\) operation addition. When addition satisfies \(\vect{u},\vect{v}\in V\implies \vect{u}+\vect{v}\in V\) (the first dot point above), we say that \(V\) is closed under addition. Similarly, when the scalar multiplication operation satisfies \(\vect{u}\in V,\, c\in\mathbb R\implies c\vect{u}\in V\text{,}\) we say that \(V\) is closed under scalar multiplication.
