Question: How many people shop at \(W\) and \(C\) in the following month?
ðŸ”—
For short, we will say that a person is in state \(W\) if they shop at \(W\) that month, and in state \(C\) if they shop at \(C\text{.}\) Then
\begin{align*} \amp \# \text{ of people in state next month} \\ \amp \qquad\qquad = 0.9(\text{# of customers}) + 0.3(\text{ of customers}) \\ \amp \qquad\qquad = 0.9(100) + 0.3(80)\\ \amp \qquad\qquad = 90 + 24 \\ \amp \qquad\qquad = 114, \end{align*}
and
\begin{align*} \amp \#\text{ of people in state next month} \\ \amp \qquad\qquad = 0.1(\text{ of customers}) + 0.7(\text{ of customers}) \\ \amp \qquad\qquad = 10 + 56 \\ \amp \qquad\qquad = 66\text{,} \end{align*}
which answers the question. Note that
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 0.9 \amp 0.3 \\ 0.1 \amp 0.7 \end{bmatrix} \colvec{100}{80} = \colvec{114}{66}, \end{equation*}
so if \(\vect{x}_0 = \colvec{100}{80}\) is the vector encoding the initial states (the amount of customers in the current month), then
\begin{equation*} \vect{x}_1 = P\vect{x}_0, \qquad \text{where } P = \begin{bmatrix} 0.9 \amp 0.3 \\ 0.1 \amp 0.7 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
is the vector encoding the states after 1 month (the next month).
