Theorem 2.3.3. (Shortest distance point to line).

In the plane, let \(P\) be a point, \(\ell\) be a line, and \(\vect{u}\) a nonzero vector parallel to \(\ell\text{.}\) Then the distance from \(P\) to \(\ell\) is \(\|\proj{u}{v} - \vect{v}\|\text{,}\) where \(\vect{v} = \overrightarrow{AP}\) and \(A\) is any point on \(\ell\text{.}\)
