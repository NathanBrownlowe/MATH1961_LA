Example 7.2.3.

Determine whether the matrix is invertible and find the inverse if it exists for the following matrices:
\begin{equation*} A= \begin{bmatrix} 2 \amp 5 \\ 1\amp 3 \end{bmatrix} , \qquad B = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp -1 \\ -2 \amp 2 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}

First, \(\det(A) = 2\times 3 - 5\times 1 = 1\text{,}\) so \(A\) is invertible, with inverse
\begin{equation*} A\inv = \frac{1}{\det(A)} \begin{bmatrix} 3 \amp -5 \\ -1 \amp 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \amp -5 \\ -1 \amp 2 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Second, \(B\) is not invertible since \(\det(B) = 1\times 2 - (-1)(-2) = 2 - 2 = 0\text{.}\)
